# Seasons

Every solstice and equinox is a celestial holiday, marking four seasons.
Solar holidays, called Festivals, last two days:
the day the celestial event occurs, and the day after.

## Spring

By the Spring Equinox,
day and night are equal once more,
and sunlight warms us more and more.
The denizens of Gaia unfurl and blossom,
shaking off Winter's chill.
To celebrate, open yourself to the world.
Accept change and growth!
Venture forth, seek out, and raise high
What warm you like sunlight.

## Summer

By the Summer Solstice,
day reaches its zenith
though night begins to take its turn.
Flowers wilt in pervasive heat;
the season of fire washes the green in red,
and the sky in smoke.
To celebrate, shed what has burned.
Relieve yourself of burdens.
Confront trauma with love.
To prune a tree is not always violence
but careful adoring care.

## Fall

By the Fall Equinox,
day and night are equal once more,
though night blankets us in moonlight.
Golden boughs shed what become sodden piles
whose rot returns to life in Gaia's blessed cycle.
To celebrate, honor dirt and decay,
the foundations of every ecosystem on Earth.
To perish is a privilege that makes living possible;
all that leaves makes room for something new.

## Winter

By the Winter Solstice,
night reaches its zenith
and day begins to take its turn.
Cold invades the body,
settling in your bones.
Trees creak in wild winds;
creatures dream in long sleep.
To celebrate, rest. Take reprieve.
Sit with stillness. Abide absence.
Eat well, stay warm, and share.
A social species survives through cooperation.
