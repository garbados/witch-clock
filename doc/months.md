# Months

There are twelve or thirteen lunar months in an annual cycle,
beginning with the Jester's Moon.
In cycles with twelve months, they are called Nomad Years;
in thirteen, Corpse Years.
The first day of each month's full moon is a celestial holiday.
Lunar holidays, called Feasts, last one day.
Each month, called a Moon, has a personality characterized by questions.

## Jester

Deep in the cold of Winter, we remember the purpose of joy.
We tell stories and jokes, and share food and laughter,
because no long night nor frigid sky can prevent happiness.
We can carry bright hearts into even the darkest realms.
How do you glow in light's absence?
How do you laugh in the green's yearly grave?

## Wizard

Beset by bitter cold, we remember the work,
especially the cleverness,
that community takes.
The roads, the houses, the meals, the medicine,
the ingenuity of invention,
which only our courage preserves against decay and entropy.

## Diviner

As night recedes and blossoms begin,
we remember the strength and spirit that community takes:
the cooperation, the faith, the care, the reflection, the growth,
and the willful choices that give love specific forms
which turn how we cherish each other into healing acts.

## Monarch

While the climate rouses its domain from wintry slumber,
we remember the splendor and sensitivity of dominion.
What are we responsible for, or want to be?
What inspires us to exert authority, and how can we administer it wisely?
What distinguishes tyranny from benevolence?
A leader is never more than their follower's equal;
the wise ruler serves.

## Steward

As resurgent ecosystems blossom and thrive,
we remember our impact and influence.
What helps us thrive?
How can one align their influence to help their world blossom?
A thing's keeper is not its master,
however outsized its impact.
As the giant in the garden,
mind your heavy step,
and tend the tender with grace.

## Hierophant

Summer rises, and its splendor runs deep with mystery.
Cycles follow patterns, but never quite the same.
What rules do you honor, abide, resent, or defy?
What order earns your protection?
What rigidity warrants its destruction?
What structure deserves to be transcended?

## Lover

Long days glow, and nests chirp with new generations.
To cherish others, is ancient.
Who, and what, do you hold dear? How do you show them so?
In partnership, we grow strong.
In parentage, we grow each other.
What in your life strengthens you
when you lend it strength?

## Courier

As Summer closes, it dawns that the cold will return.
The journey may be perilous,
however many times you have already traveled it.
What task calls you? How will you prepare?
How far will you go to deliver what you must?
What impedes your quest,
and how will you overcome?

## Warrior

The Earth preparing for Winter, flexes.
Its strength exerts, achieves, and transforms.
Strength takes many forms,
physical and mental and emotional and spiritual,
and more and more and more.
What settles conflict is rarely force;
bravery is not without fear,
but charges despite it.
What are your strengths? How do you use them?
How will you hone them?

## Hermit

Alone with oneself, our singularity teems.
Do you feel safe with yourself, or whole?
How does it feel to be you? How do you want it to feel?
The individual is a comforting illusion;
no one is self-made, and even the self is not singular.
When you hold community with yourself,
are you a good neighbor?

## Trader

Fortune comes in many kinds.
Everyone needs something, and nothing is without cost.
Every risk carries an opportunity.
Wealth is a form of relation;
only fools mistake it for money.
What fortunes have come to you?
What sorts of wealth do you desire?
What would you trade, to have what you want?

## Nomad

The brave wanderer endures long nights.
Will you face the unknown with wisdom, or be cowed by questions?
Life takes us many places; we travel even without moving.
Where have you been? Where do you want to go?
What feels like home?
Can it be found, or must it be made?

## Corpse

A lengthy cycle exhausts the spirit;
it dreams of sleeping as peacefully as the dead.
Everything ends, and some things never happen.
What have you not let yourself miss? What do you need to grieve for?
What drives you to endure?
Whatever it is, cherish it,
but remember:
this too shall pass.
