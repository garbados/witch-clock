# Cycle

## Years

A full lunisolar year is called a *cycle*. It begins on the first day of the first new moon.
Cycles with 12 months are called *nomadic cycles*, while those with 13 months are called *lingering cycles*.

## The Conclusion

The day before the first day of a new cycle is called *the Conclusion.*
If the last cycle had 12 months, it is called *the Return*.
If the last cycle had 13 months, it is called *the Demise*.

## The Return

An end of wandering, a time of solidarity and compassion.
The wanderer comes home, or finds one, or builds one.
Reflect on how you have changed, and the journey that remains.

## The Demise

An end of suffering, a time of loss and grief. The dead return to soil, the gone live on in memory.
Reflect on mortality, ephemerality, and longing.

## Respite

The period between the winter solstice and the cycle's conclusion is known as *Respite*.
It is bad luck to work too hard during this period, and good luck to eat well.
They say that Luna grieves for Terra, who sleeps a dreamless slumber.
They say Terra looks up at her night-pale lover, and worries the same.
