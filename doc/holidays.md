# Holidays

The Calendar of the Witchmothers observes many holidays
to celebrate the cycles of Creation.
The first day of each full moon,
each solstice and equinox,
the last day of the annual cycle,
are all holidays.
As you are able,
honor them with festivities.

## Feast of the Jester

The fool sees things differently.
The wilder brings the wild;
the wanderer enters it.
Reflect on the unexpected;
surprise someone,
especially yourself.

## Feast of the Wizard

Great feats underpin your firmament.
A vast history produces you.
Every tool is art;
Every cleverness takes creativity.
Rid yourself of something advanced
To appreciate its absence.

## Feast of the Diviner

The other, the unknown, are divine.
Community connects us to ourselves
through friends and neighbors.
Celebrate connection with joy;
gather the many and commune in love.

## Feast of the Monarch

Authority does not necessitate hierarchy;
the wise ruler serves.
Even a follower must take charge
when holding the leader to account.
Power without responsibility is ruthless,
so disarm yourself. Doff thy crown.
Ponder the pauper's perspective on privilege.

## Feast of the Steward

We move with what moves us:
destruction and creation share a step;
the herd tramples and tills alike.
To steward is not to own or control
but to neighbor, to stand with.
All that you control, controls you.
Give and take? No.
Receive and receive alike.

## Feast of the Hierophant

Creation may show indifference,
but it does not lack for rules.
Cold physics seat a sturdy throne
though its meticulous might
abides so much.
Today, damn the rules!
Question them to oblivion;
puzzle a new order in their absence.

## Feast of the Lover

You exist because Gaia loves Creation.
Is love not the cause of all life?
Love can take so many forms:
Compassion and kindness;
jealousy and rage;
brutal and violent.
Celebrate the love that fuels you;
be rid of the love that binds you.

## Feast of the Courier

Your ancestors have come so far
to live through you now.
You carry them, and all you care for
with every step you take,
with every sound you make,
with every thought you think.
Celebrate going the distance:
send a letter, make a call,
visit a grave; go the extra mile.

## Feast of the Warrior

The warrior exists for war.
Not a hunter, not a fighter,
discipline distinguishes the warrior.
A toolkit of tactics; salvos of strategies.
Sun Tzu emphasizes: the core of war
is logistical.
It is not how you fight,
but how you feed an army.
Today, feed an army
of friends and neighbors and kith and kin,
as many as you can.
Who knows when they will have to make war?

## Feast of the Hermit

Even a crowd can be a lonely place.
What connects us? What separates us?
Celebrate solitude and stand apart.
Sit alone, or with the lonely.
A social species survives together;
nothing but Creation might be self-made,
however much it seems to exist alone.

## Feast of the Trader

The hoarder's gold means nothing
in an empty bazaar.
To trade takes having and wanting;
giving so you know they will give in turn,
whether or not it profits you now.
In jubilee, forgive debts,
spend face to settle grievances,
right wrongs with your true wealth.

## Feast of the Nomad

It takes courage to wander.
The unknown may scare you,
but do you know yourself?
Step beyond your comfort,
beyond the borders you call home
whether physical, social, or mental.
Grace the world with your strangeness
and accept the strangeness you find.

## Feast of the Corpse

Do the dead sleep peacefully?
Gaia and the Creator separate when we rot,
however much rot becomes life, their love.
The cycle of decay is the mechanism by which
this world is alive.
All that passes returns changed.
Today, put to rest what needs burying.

## Festival of Spring

Blossoms and sprouts triumph!
Cold cannot halt their return.
Shake off your own frost,
shake the cruft of hibernation,
clean and cleanse and cry,
and then, in whatever way you can,
let the rhythm of the world
move you to dance.

## Festival of Summer

Long days and warm nights clothe us now.
The hot sun and cool dark shake hands
and begin to trade their domain.
Cherish the light while you can;
give gifts while your lovers yet live.
Revel raucously through the latest of annual dusks.
Eat well, laugh loudly, and spite the haters;
who has time to mind their binds?

## Festival of Fall

While the bitter cold has yet to arrive,
treasure the gold of bough and horizon alike.
The best things in life are the hardest;
tribulation takes us through winters of spirit.
Honor the growing domain of night
and prepare while you can.
Gather and share, ensure plenitude!
The individual wilts alone;
the community thrives together.

## Festival of Winter

In the frigid pale, night's dominion slips.
Second by second, the promise of dawn returns.
Dawn by dawn, seconds at a time, daylight blooms
like all the green to come,
promised by Gaia's will to live.
Share in her commitment,
her defiance of cold intertia.
The cause of life is love;
let it warm you down to the spirit.
Celebrate! Share it with all you can.

## Respite

In the quiet at the cycle's end, rest.
Weakness is the body asking;
sickness is the body demanding.
Honor that it does you the kindness
of hosting your spirit through everything,
through joy and agony,
through delight and misery,
through satiation and starvation,
down to your very last seconds alive.
Honor it with reprieves.

## The Return

A short cycle brings the wanderer home.
Prodigal stranger, share your tales!
O, how the wild bolsters you.
O, what treasures the unknown hides.
O, the secrets we leave to the shadows.
Share tales of what made you;
notice when you feel seen in what you hear.
Little separates us, ultimately.
Someday you too will be the wanderer
coming home.

## The Demise

The weary beg for rest after a long cycle,
but the dead beg for nothing.
The cycle takes them, divides the union
of Gaia and Creator that caused them.
Absence makes the heart grow fonder,
and while anything rots on Earth,
their love will be born again.
Grieve for the gone;
prepare the welcome for those to arrive.
